Get licensed

The FCC offers three levels of licensing for ham radio operators: Technician, General, and Extra class license. Members of the Oro Valley Club have offered to teach a class to prepare students for the 35 questions found on the Technician license test and can also administer the test at the Northwest campus. A one-time fee of $35 is paid to the FCC once you pass the Technician test, and a $15 fee is paid to the Volunteer Examiners at the Northwest Campus for taking any or all of the tests. There are several excellent online resources to learn about Ham radio operations and the test, including some videos below, but one of the best resources is HamStudy.org. The website is completely free to use. The site offers free practice tests, study guides, and an iOS and Android app for a small fee for studying offline and on the go.

If you are interested in signing up for a class taught by the Oro Valley club members, please sign up for the club on the Pima Engage website and contact us for more information.

The following videos are a great place to get satiated. The Ham Radio Crash Course is a popular YouTube channel and podcast that can assist you in learning more about the test and the information you need.

If you have purchased a radio and just want to jump in and learn how to use one, this video is a great quick start to using inexpensive Baofeng radios. The club has one or two radios that students can use at club meetings to scan and listen to radio commuications.

In addition to the three Ham radio licenses, a fourth option is the GMSR license for radios that operate with lower power. This license does not require testing but does require a fee paid to the FCC. If you plan on getting a Ham radio license, this process might be a good place to start, as it will open your account with the FCC and prepare you for the sign-up process on the FCC’s website.
